# Buff Cycle — Self-Buff Prologue → Group-Buff Fan-Out → Blessing / Seal / Aura Matrix → Reagent Gating → Buff Refresh on Expiry → Salvation `-30%` Threat for DPS → Combat-Only vs OOC Gating → Snapshot Contract → Cancellation Sequencing

Pre-pull and intra-fight buff-application sub-cycle. Where [melee-rotation.md](./melee-rotation.md) drives the swing/cooldown ladder, [cast-spell.md](./cast-spell.md) drives any single cast, [pull-target.md](./pull-target.md) drives pre-engage, [threat-management.md](./threat-management.md) governs who the mob chases, and [heal-task.md](./heal-task.md) governs heal-target selection, **buff-cycle** is the cross-bot policy that decides *which* auras every party member should be carrying at all times, *which* of the caster's self-buffs preempt the first pull, *which* group-buffs to fan out across the party (and on whom), and how to refresh them as they expire. This doc picks up exactly where [threat-management.md R6](./threat-management.md) leaves off (the healer wait-for-threat gate references Blessing of Salvation as the explicit `-30%` threat aura for DPS that reduces the healer-aggro window), where [heal-task.md R12](./heal-task.md) leaves off (the snapshot contract for healer state is extended here with `buff.player.has_blessing_of_salvation`, `buff.party.fortitude_targets_count`, `buff.player.aspect_active`), and where [threat-management.md R1](./threat-management.md) introduces school-based `m_threatModifier[]` (the canonical destination of `SPELL_AURA_MOD_THREAT` per-aura mutation in [`Unit::ApplyTotalThreatModifier`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Unit.cpp) line 7547).

The shipped runtime split is: per-profile **BuffTask** lives in 27/27 BotProfiles at `BotProfiles/<Spec>/Tasks/BuffTask.cs` and derives `BotTask` directly (NOT `CombatRotationTask`) — the contract is registered via `BotBase.CreateBuffTask(IBotContext)` at [`BotProfiles/Common/BotBase.cs:12`](../../../BotProfiles/Common/BotBase.cs). Per-spec buff lists ship today: DruidBalance / DruidFeral / DruidRestoration apply `MarkOfTheWild + Thorns (+OmenOfClarity for Balance)` at [`BotProfiles/DruidBalance/Tasks/BuffTask.cs`](../../../BotProfiles/DruidBalance/Tasks/BuffTask.cs); HunterBeastMastery applies `AspectOfTheHawk` at [`BotProfiles/HunterBeastMastery/Tasks/BuffTask.cs`](../../../BotProfiles/HunterBeastMastery/Tasks/BuffTask.cs); MageArcane / MageFire / MageFrost apply `ArcaneIntellect + (MageArmor | IceArmor | FrostArmor) + DampenMagic` at [`BotProfiles/MageFire/Tasks/BuffTask.cs`](../../../BotProfiles/MageFire/Tasks/BuffTask.cs) (Mage Armor preferred when available); PriestShadow applies `PowerWordFortitude` party-fan-out at [`BotProfiles/PriestShadow/Tasks/BuffTask.cs`](../../../BotProfiles/PriestShadow/Tasks/BuffTask.cs) (iterates `PartyMembers.First(x => !x.HasBuff(PowerWordFortitude))`); WarlockAffliction / WarlockDemonology / WarlockDestruction apply `DemonArmor` (fallback `DemonSkin`) at [`BotProfiles/WarlockAffliction/Tasks/BuffTask.cs`](../../../BotProfiles/WarlockAffliction/Tasks/BuffTask.cs). Healer specs (PaladinHoly, PriestHoly, ShamanRestoration) inline the **buff prologue** inside `PvERotationTask.PerformCombatRotation()` rather than via `BuffTask`: PriestHoly:27-32 (`PowerWordShield` + `InnerFire` cast-on-self prologue at every rotation entry); PaladinHoly:37 (`DevotionAura` + later `SealOfTheCrusader`); PaladinProtection:37-43 (`RighteousFury + DevotionAura + SealOfTheCrusader + SealOfRighteousness + HolyShield`); ShamanRestoration:45 (`ManaSpringTotem` proximity maintenance). DruidRestoration relies on the separate `BuffTask`.

The server-side state machine for buff application is owned by `Aura::Aura()` + `Aura::ApplyModifier` + the spell-effect dispatch table at [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuras.cpp:73`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuras.cpp) (function pointer table indexed by `SPELL_AURA_*`). The single canonical surface for **threat-modifying buffs** is `SPELL_AURA_MOD_THREAT = 10` at [`SpellAuraDefines.h:84`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuraDefines.h) dispatched to `Aura::HandleModThreat` at [`SpellAuras.cpp:4121-4152`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuras.cpp). The handler iterates `MAX_SPELL_SCHOOL` and applies `ApplyPercentModFloatVar(target->m_threatModifier[x], m_modifier.m_amount, apply)` for every school bit set in `m_miscvalue` — so a single Blessing of Salvation (spell id 1038, all schools, `m_amount = -30`) drops the bot's `m_threatModifier[school]` for all 7 schools to `0.70f`. That multiplier is consumed by `Unit::ApplyTotalThreatModifier` at [`Unit.cpp:7542-7547`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Unit.cpp) (`return threat * m_threatModifier[school]`) and called from `Unit::AddThreat` at [`Unit.cpp:7552`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Unit.cpp) — i.e., **every threat-generating action (damage, heal, debuff) by the buffed bot has its threat output multiplied by 0.70 before insertion into `ThreatManager::addThreat`**. This is the canonical `-30%` threat reduction for DPS that [threat-management.md R6](./threat-management.md) defers to here. Greater Blessing of Sanctuary (`spell 25899`) ships in TBC (2.0.1, Jan 2007) — **NOT in 1.12.1**; the 1.12.1 Paladin blessing matrix is `Kings / Might / Wisdom / Salvation / Light` only.

Reagent gating runs through [`Spell.cpp:7852-7860`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/Spell.cpp) (`Spell::CheckItems` pre-cast reagent presence check) and [`Spell.cpp:5615-5621`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/Spell.cpp) (`Spell::TakeReagents` consumption on successful cast). Power Word: Fortitude (spell 1243 / ranks 1244-2791-25389) requires a **Holy Candle** (item 17029) until rank 6 and **Sacred Candle** (item 17028) thereafter; Mark of the Wild (spell 1126 / ranks) requires a **Wild Berries / Wild Thornroot / Wild Quillvine / Wild Spineleaf** progression. Bot rotations do NOT inspect reagent inventory before dispatching `CastSpell` today — the server emits `SPELL_FAILED_ITEM_NOT_READY` (`SpellDefines.h`) on cast and the rotation continues without applying the buff. This is the dominant silent-failure mode for the PriestShadow party-fortitude fan-out when reagents are unstocked (see [#Failure modes](#failure-modes)).

The closest existing live-test anchor is `BotRunner.Tests.LiveValidation.BuffAndConsumableTests` (covers item-applied buffs only — Elixir of Lion's Strength via `CMSG_USE_ITEM`, not spell-applied buffs). Per [Plan/Activities/combat.md §BuffTask](../../Plan/Activities/combat.md), the planned `BuffTaskTests::Buff_AppliesAllSelfBuffs_BeforeFirstPull` anchor is **`not-started`**.

## Overview

Buffs in 1.12.1 are **server-side auras** applied via `CMSG_CAST_SPELL` with the target's GUID (`TARGET_FLAG_UNIT` for party-buffs, `TARGET_FLAG_SELF = 0x0000` for self-buffs — same protocol as a heal per [cast-spell.md](./cast-spell.md)). The spell's effect resolves on the server via `Spell::EffectApplyAura` (`SPELL_EFFECT_APPLY_AURA` id 6), which constructs an `Aura` object, inserts it into the target's `m_Auras` `AuraMap`, and dispatches the per-`SPELL_AURA_*` handler from the table at [`SpellAuras.cpp:64-285`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuras.cpp) (236 handler entries). For threat-modifying buffs the relevant handler is `Aura::HandleModThreat` at [`SpellAuras.cpp:4121`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellAuras.cpp) (line 4148-4151: `if (target->GetTypeId() == TYPEID_PLAYER) for (int8 x = 0; x < MAX_SPELL_SCHOOL; x++) if (m_modifier.m_miscvalue & int32(1 << x)) ApplyPercentModFloatVar(target->m_threatModifier[x], m_modifier.m_amount, apply);`).

`BuffTask` derives `BotTask` directly (NOT `CombatRotationTask`). Its `Update()` body is per-profile and follows a uniform shape: (1) **pop-when-done** at the head (`if all-buffs-present || all-buffs-on-cooldown → BotTasks.Pop(); return;`); (2) **iterate the buff list** in priority order, dispatching `ObjectManager.CastSpell(name, castOnSelf: <true if self>)` for the first missing buff that `IsSpellReady()`. The DruidBalance representative ([`BotProfiles/DruidBalance/Tasks/BuffTask.cs:9-31`](../../../BotProfiles/DruidBalance/Tasks/BuffTask.cs)) ships the canonical shape:

```csharp
public void Update()
{
    if ((ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(MarkOfTheWild) || !ObjectManager.IsSpellReady(MarkOfTheWild)) &&
        (ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(Thorns) || !ObjectManager.IsSpellReady(Thorns)) &&
        (ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(OmenOfClarity) || !ObjectManager.IsSpellReady(OmenOfClarity)))
    {
        BotTasks.Pop();
        return;
    }
    if (!ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(MarkOfTheWild))
    {
        if (ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(MoonkinForm))
            ObjectManager.CastSpell(MoonkinForm);  // shapeshift cancel — Mark of the Wild requires HumanForm
        ObjectManager.CastSpell(MarkOfTheWild);
    }
    ObjectManager.CastSpell(Thorns);
    ObjectManager.CastSpell(OmenOfClarity);
}
```

The **Paladin Blessing matrix** is structurally distinct enough to warrant standalone treatment. The five 1.12.1 Paladin blessings — `BlessingOfKings` (+10% all stats), `BlessingOfMight` (+AP), `BlessingOfWisdom` (+mp5), `BlessingOfSalvation` (-30% threat), `BlessingOfLight` (heal coefficient buff) — are **mutually exclusive per caster per target** (only one blessing from a given Paladin can be on a given target at a time; multiple Paladins can layer different blessings). The **Greater Blessings** (GreaterBlessingOfKings/Might/Wisdom/Salvation/Light, reagent: Symbol of Kings 21177) ship in patch 1.10 — they ARE available in 1.12.1 as 15-minute raid-wide buffs (vs the 5-minute single-target regular blessings). The `Spellbook.cs` constants today ship ONLY `BlessingOfKings:113`, `BlessingOfMight:114`, `BlessingOfSanctuary:115` — and `BlessingOfSanctuary` is the original 1.12.1 30%-block-chance proc buff (`spell 20911`, NOT the TBC `25899` damage-reduction). `BlessingOfWisdom`, `BlessingOfSalvation`, `BlessingOfLight`, and the five Greater variants are **NOT in Spellbook.cs today** (open Spellbook gap; see [#Failure modes](#failure-modes)).

**Aspect / Form mutual-exclusion**. Hunter Aspects (`AspectOfTheHawk`, `AspectOfTheMonkey`, `AspectOfTheCheetah`, `AspectOfTheWolf`-TBC, `AspectOfTheWild`, `AspectOfTheBeast`-vanilla, `AspectOfThePack`-vanilla) are mutually exclusive — applying a new one removes the old via `SPELL_AURA_TRACK_CREATURES` and friends (handled in the `Aura::HandleAura*` family). Druid shapeshift forms (`BearForm`, `CatForm`, `MoonkinForm`, `HumanForm`) likewise cancel each other. The DruidBalance BuffTask's MoonkinForm-cancel at line 21-24 (`if (ObjectManager.Player.HasBuff(MoonkinForm)) ObjectManager.CastSpell(MoonkinForm);` — casting again toggles off) is the sequencing-cancel pattern. The Warrior stance trinity (`BattleStance / DefensiveStance / BerserkerStance`) similarly cancels — but is NOT a buff in the conventional sense (no `BuffTask` integration; stance switches happen in-rotation per WarriorProtection:60-61).

**Combat-only vs OOC**. `SPELL_ATTR_NOT_IN_COMBAT_ONLY_PEACEFUL = 0x10000000` at [`SpellDefines.h:741`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Spells/SpellDefines.h) marks "cannot be used in combat" — applies to peace-time-only buffs like Hearthstone. The reverse — "in-combat only" — uses the `SPELL_FAILED_AFFECTING_COMBAT` result code (line 285 `SpellDefines.h`). Most prologue buffs (Mark of the Wild, Inner Fire, Blessings, Power Word: Fortitude) can be cast in OR out of combat. Inner Focus (Priest, spell 14751) is "next cast 0-mana" — combat-only utility. Power Word: Shield (Priest) IS combat-usable. Warriors' BattleShout (`+strength + AP` party-buff, 2-min duration) is in-combat OK; rage cost (10 rage) is the OOC gating practical mechanism (without aggro, only Bloodrage → BattleShout sequence works pre-pull).
