# Spirit Healer Extras — Resurrection Sickness, Durability Cascade, Cost-Benefit, BG Overrides, Class Bypasses, Wisp Form

Companion doc to [`spirit-healer.md`](./spirit-healer.md). Where the parent doc isolates the **`CMSG_SPIRIT_HEALER_ACTIVATE` round-trip** as a first-class Activity surface for the planned `SpiritHealerTask`, this doc collects the **adjacent mechanics** that the parent doc only touches in passing — and that the Decision-Engine must reason about when picking between `SpiritHealerTask` and its sibling branches. Iter-25 [`innkeeper.md`](../npc/innkeeper.md) worker recommendation: "Direct cross-cite with `gossip.md` `GOSSIP_OPTION_SPIRITHEALER = 6` and `innkeeper.md` R13 (Hearth as recovery-finish). Low risk, high cross-pollination with existing docs." This doc closes that loop.

Six adjacent mechanic families are documented here:

1. **Resurrection sickness mechanics** — spell `15007`, level-gated duration, `-75% stats / -75% damage / -50% movement` while active; the **largest XP-grind blocker** the bot can self-inflict.
2. **25% durability cost cascade** — `DurabilityLossAll(0.25f, true)` and the inevitable flip into `RepairTask` when items cross the broken threshold.
3. **Corpse-run vs spirit-healer cost-benefit** — gold-loss vs time-loss decision; the engine's "I am hopeless" branch from [`spirit-healer.md`](./spirit-healer.md) R13.
4. **BG-zone spirit-healer overrides** — different rules in battlegrounds (`CMSG_AREA_SPIRIT_HEALER_QUERY`, 30s controlled-GY tick, no durability tax / no sickness on the BG resurrect path).
5. **Class-specific bypasses** — Paladin Lay-on-Hands (self-heal precludes some death cases) + Priest Spirit-of-Redemption talent + Shaman Reincarnation (item-reagent self-rez) + Night Elf wisp form.
6. **Cross-NPC interactions** — `GOSSIP_OPTION_SPIRITHEALER = 6` gossip cascade + post-spirit-heal `RepairTask + RestTask + InnkeeperBind` recovery-finish chain ending at [`innkeeper.md`](../npc/innkeeper.md) R13 (Hearth back to bind).

The parent doc's R6/R7/R8/R12/R15 each have an "extra mechanics" footprint that didn't fit the parent's already-large rule table; this doc is where those footprints land.

## Overview

**Resurrection sickness (Spell 15007).** Server-side cast at [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp:5138-5158`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp) inside `ResurrectPlayer(restorePercent, applySickness)` — only when `applySickness == true` (which `SendSpiritResurrect` sets unconditionally at [`NPCHandler.cpp:452`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/NPCHandler.cpp)) AND `GetLevel() >= CONFIG_INT32_DEATH_SICKNESS_LEVEL` (default 11). Spell ID source: `SPELL_ID_PASSIVE_RESURRECTION_SICKNESS = 15007` at [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/SharedDefines.h:1170`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/SharedDefines.h). Per-race override possible via `ChrRacesEntry.resSicknessSpellId` at [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Database/DBCStructure.h:171`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Database/DBCStructure.h) — comment notes "blizzlike always 15007", so engine should treat the value as a constant for 1.12.1 vanilla but should NOT hardcode (per-race override field exists in DBC). Duration formula at [`Player.cpp:5148-5156`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp): for `level ∈ [startLevel, startLevel + 9)`, duration = `(level - startLevel + 1) * MINUTE * IN_MILLISECONDS`; at `level >= startLevel + 9` (i.e. L20+), the spell's default duration (10 min full) applies. Mechanically the aura applies multiple negative modifiers (per the underlying spell entry — see [Player.cpp resurrect block](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp) for the cast point and `SpellAuraDefines.h:167` (`SPELL_AURA_MOD_STAT = 29`) + `SpellAuraDefines.h:217` (`SPELL_AURA_MOD_DAMAGE_PERCENT_DONE = 79`) for the aura families it uses): roughly `-75% to all stats`, `-75% to damage done`, `-75% to healing done`, `-50% to movement speed`. While active, **XP grind is functionally impossible** — mob TTK rises 4× and bot survival drops sharply.

**Durability cascade.** Spirit-healer activate runs `DurabilityLossAll(0.25f, true)` at [`NPCHandler.cpp:454`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/NPCHandler.cpp); body at [`Player.cpp:5257-5298`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp). The `true` second arg extends the loss to **inventory items, not just equipped slots** — every bag item with `pMaxDurability > 0` drops 25% (`pDurabilityLoss = uint32(pMaxDurability * 0.25)` at [`Player.cpp:5293`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp)). Compare with on-death `DurabilityLossAll(0.10f, false)` at [`Player.cpp:1166`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp) — death alone burns 10% equipped-only; spirit-healer **adds** an additional 25% on top of that 10%, AND extends to inventory. A bot that dies once + spirit-heals is at `equipped: 67.5% / inventory: 75%` durability immediately. Two such cycles back-to-back put most equipped items into `red` (50% threshold) and chain-deaths can push items to `broken` (0%) which removes their stat contribution server-side. **The 25% loss is the dominant economic cost of spirit-healer use** — gold-amortised over typical repair vendor rates, ~50-200c per use at low level, escalating to multi-gold at endgame.

**Corpse-run-vs-spirit-healer decision.** Parent doc [`spirit-healer.md`](./spirit-healer.md) R13 establishes the "I am hopeless" threshold: corpse > 200 yd OR `RetrieveCorpseTask` returned `NoPathTimeout` (≥ 30 s) ≥ 3× OR `stuck-recovery.md` R14 escalated to L3+ in ghost form. The corpse-run path costs **time only** (no durability loss, no sickness — corpse-reclaim restores HP/mana to 50% via `restore_percent = 0.5f` at [`MiscHandler.cpp:663`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/MiscHandler.cpp)), while spirit-healer costs **gold + grind time**. Below L11, sickness doesn't apply and spirit-healer is "just" a 25% durability tax — corpse-run vs spirit-healer is closer to break-even when corpse is reachable in `< 90s` walk. Above L11, the sickness penalty makes corpse-run dominant on every metric except wall-clock — only choose spirit-healer when corpse is genuinely unreachable.

**Battleground overrides.** BG resurrect path is **different from PvE spirit-healer**. The BG-only `CMSG_AREA_SPIRIT_HEALER_QUERY` opcode at [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/BattleGroundHandler.cpp:611-628`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/BattleGroundHandler.cpp) and `CMSG_AREA_SPIRIT_HEALER_QUEUE` at [`BattleGroundHandler.cpp:630-647`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/BattleGroundHandler.cpp) reach a different code path than PvE: the BG queues the player at the area spirit healer (`SendAreaSpiritHealerQueryOpcode` on [`Creature.cpp:3484+`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Creature.cpp)) and the actual resurrect fires on a **30-second controlled-GY tick** rather than on-demand. The BG resurrect path applies neither the 25% durability loss nor resurrection sickness (which is the deliberate design — BGs are about repeat engagements at fast cadence; sickness would break the cadence). BG resurrects use `restore_percent = 1.0f` per [`MiscHandler.cpp:663`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/MiscHandler.cpp) (full HP/mana). Bot's `SpiritHealerTask` must distinguish via `player.InBattleGround` snapshot before dispatch — wrong-path dispatch (`CMSG_SPIRIT_HEALER_ACTIVATE` to a BG area-spirit-healer when `IsSpiritService() == true` per [`BattleGroundHandler.cpp:624`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Handlers/BattleGroundHandler.cpp)) is silently rejected and the bot waits indefinitely. Parent doc [`spirit-healer.md`](./spirit-healer.md) R12 names this branch; this doc's R5 fills in the rest.

**Class bypasses.** Three native 1.12.1 class abilities partially or fully bypass the spirit-healer code path: (a) Paladin Lay-on-Hands (spell 633, restores 100% HP, can be cast self in 1.12.1 — usable to prevent the death entirely, not bypass post-death); (b) Priest Spirit of Redemption (talent spell 20711 — 15-second ghost-form window after death where the priest can still heal allies, per [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Unit.cpp:1066`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Unit.cpp) — Priest with this talent in spec gets an extra window before fully dying); (c) **Shaman Reincarnation** (passive spell 20608 + active spell 21169 — the only true class self-rez in 1.12.1, requires Ankh reagent itemId 17030, per [`D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp:20270-20272`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp)). Reincarnation grants 20% HP/mana and **no sickness** when the Ankh is consumed — strictly superior to spirit-healer for Shamans with Ankhs in inventory. Engine must check shaman+Ankh+spell-ready before defaulting to corpse-run / spirit-healer.

**Night Elf wisp form.** NE-only spell 20584 ([`Player.cpp:5049-5050`](D:/MaNGOS/source/src/game/Objects/Player.cpp)) gives the ghost form a +50% movement-speed buff (`SPELL_AURA_INCREASE_SPEED + SPELL_AURA_INCREASE_SWIM_SPEED + SPELL_AURA_TRANSFORM`). NE ghosts run-back to corpse 1.5× faster than other races — shifts the corpse-run-vs-spirit-healer break-even point. For NE, prefer corpse-run even on routes that would push other races toward spirit-healer; the 50% speed makes 300 yd corpse-runs feasible inside 60 s.

**Cross-NPC gossip cascade.** `GOSSIP_OPTION_SPIRITHEALER = 6` per [`gossip.md`](../npc/gossip.md) is the gossip-overlay path equivalent to `CMSG_SPIRIT_HEALER_ACTIVATE`. Some non-spirit-healer NPCs (e.g. spirit guides in starting zones) carry the gossip option without the `UNIT_NPC_FLAG_SPIRITHEALER = 0x20` bit — these gate on the gossip-row selection rather than the direct opcode. Bot's `SpiritHealerTask` should prefer the direct opcode path (no gossip-overlay round-trip) when the NPC carries `0x20`, falling back to gossip-selection only when only `GOSSIP_OPTION_SPIRITHEALER` is exposed. Post-spirit-heal recovery-finish chain typically ends at an [`innkeeper.md`](../npc/innkeeper.md) — Hearth back to bind (innkeeper.md R13) is the "I'm at a far graveyard, get me home" reset that closes most spirit-healer recoveries.
